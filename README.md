It's an open source course based on the curriculum for the oxford university MSc in Mathematical and Computational Finance


# Subjects

## Term 1

- Stochastic Calculus
-- Overview:
Stochastic calculus is a branch of mathematics that deals with processes that have a random component. It's widely used in financial mathematics for modeling the random behavior of asset prices and interest rates.

-- Core Topics:

### Introduction to Stochastic Processes

Random walks, Brownian motion, and Markov processes.
Conditional expectations and martingales.
Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs)

Ito's Lemma and its applications.
SDEs in financial modeling: Black-Scholes model.
Numerical Solutions of SDEs

Euler-Maruyama method.
Milstein method for higher accuracy.
Measure Theory and Probability

Sigma-algebras and measurable functions.
Probability measures and integration.
Financial Applications

Derivative pricing and hedging strategies.
Risk-neutral valuation.
Recommended Textbooks:

"Stochastic Calculus for Finance I: The Binomial Asset Pricing Model" by Steven E. Shreve.
"Stochastic Calculus for Finance II: Continuous-Time Models" by Steven E. Shreve.
"Measure, Integral and Probability" by Marek Capinski and Ekkehard Kopp.
Practical Exercises:

Model asset prices using Brownian motion and random walks.
Implement the Euler-Maruyama method to solve SDEs.
Analyze the martingale properties in financial contexts.
Free Online Courses:

Coursera: Stochastic Processes

MIT OpenCourseWare: Stochastic Processes

Financial derivatives are financial instruments whose value is derived from the value of underlying assets. They are used for hedging risks, speculating, and arbitraging opportunities in financial markets.

Core Topics:

Introduction to Derivatives

Types: Options, futures, swaps.
Applications: Hedging, speculation, arbitrage.
Option Pricing Models

Black-Scholes model and binomial tree model.
Greek Letters and Risk Management

Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega, Rho.
Hedging strategies.
Exotic Options and Structured Products

Asian, Barrier, Lookback options.
Interest Rate Derivatives

Models for pricing bonds and interest rate swaps.
Caplets, floorlets, and swaptions.
Recommended Textbooks:

"Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives" by John C. Hull.
"The Concepts and Practice of Mathematical Finance" by Mark S. Joshi.
"Introduction to the Mathematics of Financial Derivatives" by Salih N. Neftci.
